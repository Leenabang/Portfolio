Hey, I'M LEENA BANG.

About Me
An UI/UX Designer based in India. I'm drawn to messy, real world problems, the kind where people are already frustrated before they even open the app. My favorite thing is taking something tangled and quietly turning it into something that just makes sense. A lot of my design instinct feels like swimming, which happens to be how I clear my head. You can't fight the water. You find the cleanest line through it and let everything else fall away. That's how I approach a confusing flow too: cut the drag, find the path of least resistance, and help people move forward without thinking about the effort.

What I do
For nearly 4 years at Hexaware, I've designed for healthcare, insurance, telecom, and warehouse teams. These are spaces where a confusing screen isn't just annoying. It can change the decision someone makes. A few problems I've untangled:

  • At WellDyne, people were missing 70% of their cost-saving options because of poor hierarchy. I rebuilt it so the right comparison showed up exactly when they needed to choose.
  • For Ricoh's AR warehouse app, workers were juggling a scanner, a spreadsheet, and a checklist at once. I folded all three into one flow, with one-handed and voice controls that cut 80% of confirmation taps.
  • At Indeez, I built a design system from scratch so handoff stopped being a headache.
  • At Vodafone Qatar, I rebuilt SIM activation into a fully digital journey, so nobody had to step into a store.

How I think about it
What matters to me isn't whether a screen looks clean. It's whether it holds up when someone's actually using it, sometimes literally walking through a warehouse with their hands full. So I start by finding where people get stuck and what can be simplified without flattening the real problem. Then I do the part I love: shaping flows, cutting friction, and making the next step feel obvious. I prototype fast and test early, so I'm fixing things in a file instead of after launch.

Where I'm headed
Right now I want to become a sharper product thinker, someone who can design a polished interface and understand the whole system humming behind it.

PORTFOLIO

Design thinking isn’t just a method; it's how I approach every project - blending creativity with strategy, empathy with data, and vision with practicality.

At the core of my design philosophy lies the principle of design thinking - a structured yet flexible process that puts the user first. It’s more than a framework; it’s a mindset that encourages empathy, creativity, and collaboration to create solutions that are not only functional but also meaningful.

Off the clock

When I step away from screens, I swim, try new cuisines or cafes, and lose myself in music. And honestly, a lot of my downtime still loops back to design. Lately I've been hooked on AI assisted tools, just to see how far a rough idea can travel before lunch.

I'm always up for talking design, new tools, or a genuinely interesting problem. If any of this clicks, please reach out to me!
